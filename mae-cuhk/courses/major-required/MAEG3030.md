# MAEG3030 Fluid Mechanics

**Course Type**: Major Required  
**Units**: 3

## Official Description
This course covers important topics in fluid mechanics. It will introduce the nature of fluids; pressure, hydrostatics, and buoyancy; integral and differential equations of fluid flows; conservation of mass, momentum, and energy; basic mass transfer; inviscid flow, viscous-dominated flows; Navier-Stokes equation; dimensional analysis; internal and external flows, boundary layers, and lift and drag on objects.

## Key Topics
- Fluid properties and hydrostatics
- Conservation laws (mass, momentum, energy)
- Navier-Stokes equations
- Dimensional analysis and similitude
- Internal flows (pipes) and external flows (boundary layers, drag/lift)
- Viscous and inviscid flows

## Learning Outcomes
- Apply conservation principles to fluid problems
- Analyze internal and external flows
- Use dimensional analysis for scaling

## Self-Study Notes & Resources
- **Textbook**: White Fluid Mechanics or Munson Fundamentals of Fluid Mechanics
- **Key Concepts**: Bernoulli equation, Reynolds number, boundary layer, drag and lift coefficients
- **Relation to Robotics / My Projects**:
  - Important for understanding fluid interactions in soft robotics and underwater/aerial robots
  - Relevant for actuator design involving fluids or aerodynamics

## Practical Application in My Simulator
- Background knowledge helpful when considering fluid-based or soft actuators in future extensions of the simulator

## Practice & Exercises
- Pipe flow and head loss calculations
- Boundary layer and drag analysis
- Dimensional analysis problems
