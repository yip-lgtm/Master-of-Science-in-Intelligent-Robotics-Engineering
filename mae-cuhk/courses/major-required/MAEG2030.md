# MAEG2030 Thermodynamics

**Course Type**: Major Required  
**Units**: 3

## Official Description
This course will cover fundamental topics in thermodynamics including basic concepts, heat and work, pure substance, ideal gas, first law and second law of thermodynamics, entropy, gas and vapor power cycles, and refrigeration cycles.

## Key Topics
- Basic concepts, systems, properties, processes
- Heat, work, and the first law
- Pure substances and ideal gases
- Second law and entropy
- Gas and vapor power cycles (Otto, Diesel, Rankine, Brayton)
- Refrigeration cycles

## Learning Outcomes
- Apply first and second laws of thermodynamics
- Analyze thermodynamic cycles
- Understand properties of pure substances and ideal gases

## Self-Study Notes & Resources
- **Textbook**: Cengel & Boles Thermodynamics
- **Key Concepts**: Energy balance, entropy generation, cycle efficiency
- **Relation to Robotics / My Projects**:
  - Relevant for thermal management in robots and actuators
  - Useful background for energy-efficient robotic systems and soft robotics (Week 3)

## Practical Application in My Simulator
- Considered thermal aspects when thinking about real-world actuator selection and power systems for physical robot builds

## Practice & Exercises
- Analyze simple power and refrigeration cycles
- Property tables and ideal gas calculations
- Cycle efficiency calculations
