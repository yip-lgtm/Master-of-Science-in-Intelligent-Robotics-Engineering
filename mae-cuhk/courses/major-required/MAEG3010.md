# MAEG3010 Mechanics of Materials

**Course Type**: Major Required  
**Units**: 3

## Official Description
This course is a study of mechanics of materials. It covers the following topics: linear elasticity, stress and strain, stress-strain relations, loading and deformation, statically indeterminate problems, torsion, shear forces and bending moments, stresses in beams, deflection of beams, stresses in thin-walled pressure vessels, column and buckling.

## Key Topics
- Stress and strain, Hooke’s law
- Torsion of circular shafts
- Shear force and bending moment diagrams
- Bending stresses and beam deflection
- Column buckling
- Thin-walled pressure vessels

## Learning Outcomes
- Analyze stress and strain in structural members
- Determine internal forces and stresses in beams and shafts
- Solve deflection and buckling problems

## Self-Study Notes & Resources
- **Textbook**: Hibbeler Mechanics of Materials
- **Key Formulas**: Bending stress σ = My/I, Torsion τ = Tr/J, Euler buckling load
