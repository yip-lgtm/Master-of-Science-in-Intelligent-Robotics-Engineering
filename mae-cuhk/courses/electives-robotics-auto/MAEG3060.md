# MAEG3060 Introduction to Robotics

**Course Type**: Major Elective (Robotics and Automation Stream)  
**Units**: 3

## Official Description
The course covers the basic and fundamentals to the study of robotic manipulators, including: classification of robots, robot sensors and actuators, coordinate frames and homogeneous transformations, Denavit-Hartenberg notation, forwards and inverse kinematics, Jacobians and statics, singularity and workspace analysis, and trajectory generation. The course includes a creative project component for students to apply robot manipulators for a new and innovative application.

## Key Topics
- Robot classification and components (sensors, actuators, end-effectors)
- Coordinate frames and homogeneous transformations (SE(3))
- Denavit-Hartenberg (DH) parameters
- Forward kinematics
- Inverse kinematics (analytical and numerical)
- Jacobians, velocity, and statics
- Singularity and workspace analysis
- Trajectory generation and planning
- Project: Innovative robot manipulator application

## Learning Outcomes
- Derive forward and inverse kinematics for serial manipulators
- Analyze robot workspace and singularities
- Plan trajectories for robot tasks
- Design and prototype simple robotic applications
