# MAEG2050 Robot Development in Practice: From Design to Prototyping

**Course Type**: Major Elective (Design and Manufacturing / Robotics Stream)  
**Units**: 3

## Official Description
This course is aimed to enhance students’ professional and engineering interests through practicum and team-based development of practical robots through participation in hands-on competitions. This is a project-based experiential course that exposes students to design and build practical robots. Students will be required to conceptualize and perform high-level design of a robot system, and then design and build various components using skills including CAD drawing, mechanical structure, electronic circuits and programming skills.

## Key Topics
- Robot system design and high-level architecture
- CAD and mechanical structure design
- Electronics and circuit integration
- Programming and control
- Prototyping and testing
- Project management and teamwork
- Participation in robot competitions or challenges

## Learning Outcomes
- Design and prototype functional robots
- Integrate mechanical, electronic, and software components
- Apply project management skills in team environment
- Solve real-world robotics problems hands-on
